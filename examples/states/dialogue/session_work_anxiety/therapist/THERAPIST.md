# Session Plan: Work Presentation Anxiety

**Dialogue ID**: `session_work_anxiety`  
**Session Number**: 5  
**Client**: Sarah (anxious professional)  
**Therapist**: Dr. Chen (CBT-oriented)

## Pre-Session Preparation

### Review from Last Session
- Sarah was working on challenging "should" statements
- Homework: Thought record on perfectionism at work
- Made progress on recognizing all-or-nothing thinking
- Alliance is strong; Sarah is engaged and motivated

### Current Presentation
- High anxiety about upcoming executive presentation (3 days away)
- Physical symptoms: headache, insomnia, GI distress
- Maladaptive coping: over-preparation, sleep deprivation
- Catastrophic thinking activated

## Session Structure (60 minutes)

### Opening (5 min)
- Check-in on current state
- Acknowledge visible distress
- Set collaborative agenda

### Homework Review (10 min)
- Review thought record from last week
- Acknowledge if homework wasn't completed (likely due to presentation stress)
- Don't spend too much time here given acute crisis

### Main Work (35 min)

**Phase 1: Validate and Assess (10 min)**
- Validate the real pressure and importance
- Assess current anxiety level and functioning
- Explore impact on sleep, relationships, self-care
- Normalize anxiety before important events

**Phase 2: Cognitive Restructuring (15 min)**
- Identify catastrophic predictions about presentation
- Use thought record in session:
  - Situation: Executive presentation in 3 days
  - Automatic thoughts: "I'll mess up and ruin my career"
  - Evidence for/against
  - Alternative thoughts
  - Re-rate anxiety
- Decatastrophizing questions:
  - "What's the worst that could realistically happen?"
  - "How likely is that?"
  - "If it did happen, how would you cope?"
  - "In 5 years, how much will this matter?"

**Phase 3: Behavioral Planning (10 min)**
- Create structured preparation plan (limit rehearsal)
- Schedule self-care (sleep, meals, exercise)
- Develop day-of coping plan
- Practice grounding technique in session

### Homework Assignment (5 min)
- Thought record on presentation catastrophizing
- Preparation schedule (max 1 hour/day)
- Self-care commitments (specific times)
- Daily grounding practice
- Optional: Practice presentation once with partner

### Closing (5 min)
- Summarize key points
- Check Sarah's understanding and buy-in
- Offer encouragement
- Remind of between-session support options if needed

## Key Therapeutic Goals

### Immediate Goals (This Session)
1. Reduce acute anxiety to manageable level
2. Challenge catastrophic thinking
3. Create concrete action plan for next 3 days
4. Restore self-care basics (especially sleep)

### Intermediate Goals (Next Few Sessions)
1. Process presentation outcome (compare predictions to reality)
2. Generalize learning to future high-stakes situations
3. Continue work on perfectionism
4. Build distress tolerance skills

### Long-Term Goals (Treatment)
1. Separate self-worth from achievement
2. Develop self-compassion
3. Tolerate imperfection and uncertainty
4. Build internal validation

## Anticipated Challenges

### Client Challenges
- **Resistance to limiting preparation**: "But I need to be ready!"
  - Response: "There's a point of diminishing returns. Over-preparation increases anxiety without improving performance."
  
- **Seeking reassurance**: "Do you think I'll do okay?"
  - Response: "I think you're well-prepared. And more importantly, what do you think?"
  
- **Minimizing self-care**: "I don't have time for that right now"
  - Response: "Sleep and self-care aren't luxuries—they're what allow your brain to perform well."

### Therapist Challenges
- Balancing validation with challenge
- Not colluding with catastrophizing
- Maintaining structure while being responsive to emotion
- Resisting urge to reassure (build her internal confidence instead)

## Therapeutic Techniques to Employ

### CBT Techniques
- **Thought records**: Structured cognitive restructuring
- **Socratic questioning**: Help her discover alternatives
- **Behavioral experiments**: "What if you only rehearse once more?"
- **Decatastrophizing**: Examine realistic worst-case scenarios
- **Cost-benefit analysis**: Over-preparation vs. self-care

### ACT Techniques
- **Values clarification**: "What matters more than a perfect presentation?"
- **Acceptance**: "Can you make room for anxiety while still preparing?"
- **Defusion**: "Notice the thought 'I'll fail' as just a thought, not a fact"

### Mindfulness Techniques
- **Grounding exercise**: Practice in session
- **Present-moment focus**: "Right now, in this moment, are you okay?"

## Interventions to Avoid

### Don't
- Give false reassurance ("You'll do great!")
- Minimize her concerns ("It's not that big a deal")
- Collude with catastrophizing ("Yes, this could ruin your career")
- Lecture about self-care without empathy
- Push too hard if she's not ready

### Do
- Validate real pressure while challenging distortions
- Acknowledge her competence while addressing anxiety
- Collaborate on solutions rather than prescribe
- Balance empathy with skill-teaching
- Maintain warm, supportive stance

## Cultural Considerations

### Relevant Factors
- First-generation college graduate (high achievement pressure)
- Tech industry culture (hustle mentality)
- Family messaging: achievement = worth
- Stigma about showing vulnerability at work

### Adaptations
- Acknowledge cultural context of achievement pressure
- Validate drive while addressing costs
- Respect work ethic while promoting balance
- Frame self-care as performance-enhancing, not weakness

## Risk Assessment

### Current Risk Level: Low
- No suicidal ideation
- No self-harm
- Functioning impaired but not severely
- Has support system (partner)
- Engaged in treatment

### Monitor For
- Worsening insomnia
- Increased physical symptoms
- Relationship strain
- Avoidance of presentation (unlikely but possible)

## Post-Session Notes

### Document
- Current anxiety level and functioning
- Catastrophic thoughts identified
- Interventions used
- Homework assigned
- Plan for follow-up after presentation

### Self-Reflection
- Did I balance validation and challenge effectively?
- Was I too structured or not structured enough?
- Did I address her emotional state adequately?
- What would I do differently?

## Follow-Up Plan

### Next Session (After Presentation)
- Process actual outcome vs. predicted outcome
- Reinforce learning about catastrophizing
- Celebrate facing fear
- Generalize to future situations
- Continue work on perfectionism and self-worth

### If Presentation Goes Well
- Acknowledge success while noting it wasn't "perfect"
- Highlight that anxiety didn't prevent good performance
- Discuss how over-preparation may not have been necessary
- Build confidence for future challenges

### If Presentation Has Difficulties
- Normalize that not everything goes perfectly
- Challenge catastrophic interpretation
- Problem-solve any real issues
- Maintain perspective (one presentation ≠ career)
- Use as learning opportunity
