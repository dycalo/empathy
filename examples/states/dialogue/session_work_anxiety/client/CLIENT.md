# Session Context: Work Presentation Anxiety

**Dialogue ID**: `session_work_anxiety`  
**Session Number**: 5 (mid-therapy)  
**Date**: Week of April 21, 2026

## Current Situation

Sarah has a major presentation to the executive team in 3 days. She's been asked to present the Q2 marketing strategy and is experiencing intense anxiety about it.

### Immediate Stressors
- **Presentation**: 30-minute presentation to C-suite executives
- **Stakes**: Her promotion review is coming up; feels this presentation is critical
- **Preparation**: Has been over-preparing, losing sleep, experiencing physical symptoms
- **Catastrophic thoughts**: "If I mess this up, I'll lose my credibility and won't get promoted"

### Recent Events (Past Week)
- Stayed up until 2am three nights in a row rehearsing
- Had a tension headache for 4 days straight
- Snapped at her partner when he suggested she was over-preparing
- Declined social plans to keep working on slides
- Checked in with her manager twice for reassurance about the presentation

## Current Emotional State

### Primary Emotions
- **Anxiety**: 8/10 intensity, constant background worry
- **Fear**: Of failure, judgment, being exposed as incompetent
- **Exhaustion**: From lack of sleep and constant stress
- **Frustration**: With herself for "being so anxious about this"

### Physical Symptoms
- Tension headache (4 days)
- Difficulty falling asleep (racing thoughts)
- Jaw clenching
- Stomach upset
- Fatigue despite caffeine

### Cognitive State
- Catastrophizing about all possible ways the presentation could go wrong
- Ruminating on past presentations where she felt she didn't do well
- Difficulty concentrating on anything else
- Seeking excessive reassurance from colleagues

## Behavioral Patterns This Week

### Maladaptive Coping
- Over-preparing (diminishing returns)
- Sleep deprivation
- Avoiding partner's concerns
- Excessive reassurance-seeking
- Neglecting self-care (skipped exercise, eating poorly)

### Adaptive Attempts
- Reached out to therapist (this session)
- Tried deep breathing (but "it's not working")
- Talked to partner about stress (though defensively)

## Session Goals

### Sarah's Stated Goals
- "I need to calm down about this presentation"
- "Help me stop catastrophizing"
- "I can't keep losing sleep like this"

### Therapeutic Goals
- Challenge catastrophic thinking about presentation
- Develop realistic assessment of stakes and outcomes
- Practice distress tolerance skills
- Address perfectionism and all-or-nothing thinking
- Create concrete plan for next 3 days (sleep, preparation, self-care)

## Relevant History

### Pattern Recognition
- This is a recurring pattern: high-stakes work event → catastrophizing → over-preparation → exhaustion → performance anxiety
- Previous presentations have gone well objectively, but Sarah focuses on minor flaws
- Perfectionism and fear of failure are core themes

### Previous Work in Therapy
- Identified cognitive distortions (catastrophizing, all-or-nothing thinking)
- Learned thought record technique
- Practiced challenging negative thoughts
- Discussed connection between self-worth and achievement
- Has not yet fully internalized that "good enough" is acceptable

## Therapeutic Approach for This Session

### Validate First
- Acknowledge the real pressure and importance of the presentation
- Validate that anxiety before important events is normal
- Recognize her exhaustion and distress

### Cognitive Work
- Use thought records to examine catastrophic predictions
- Examine evidence for and against "this will ruin my career"
- Decatastrophize: "What's the worst that could realistically happen?"
- Challenge all-or-nothing thinking: "Does it have to be perfect?"

### Behavioral Interventions
- Create structured preparation plan (limit rehearsal time)
- Schedule self-care activities (sleep, exercise, meals)
- Practice presentation once in session (exposure)
- Develop coping plan for day-of anxiety

### Skill Building
- Distress tolerance techniques for acute anxiety
- Grounding exercises for pre-presentation nerves
- Self-compassion practice

## Potential Challenges in Session

### Client Resistance
- May resist limiting preparation ("But I need to be ready!")
- May intellectualize rather than feel emotions
- May seek reassurance rather than building internal confidence
- May minimize need for self-care ("I don't have time")

### Therapist Considerations
- Balance validation with gentle challenge
- Don't collude with catastrophizing
- Help her see pattern without shaming
- Maintain structure while being flexible to her emotional state

## Homework for This Session

### Likely Assignments
1. **Thought record**: Track catastrophic thoughts about presentation, challenge them
2. **Preparation schedule**: Limit rehearsal to 1 hour/day max
3. **Self-care plan**: 7+ hours sleep, one meal with partner, 20-min walk
4. **Grounding practice**: 5 minutes of deep breathing daily
5. **Values reflection**: Write about why this matters beyond performance

## Follow-Up Considerations

### Next Session (After Presentation)
- Process how presentation actually went
- Compare predictions to reality
- Reinforce learning about catastrophizing
- Generalize to future high-stakes situations
- Celebrate courage in facing fear

### Long-Term Themes
- Separating self-worth from achievement
- Developing self-compassion
- Tolerating imperfection
- Building internal validation vs. external
